What is D&D?

Dungeons and Dragons, also known as D&D is an adventure roleplaying game that takes place between player(s) and their DM (Dungeon master). For each game session, the DM will have a chance to set the scene and tell the players what is going on and the players relay to the DM what they'd like to do.

Where would I start?

There are lots of starting options. You can of course by the main books off of Amazon or practically any bookstore or use an online service such as D&D beyond, but the best way to learn is to play with others. Try and find a group that you can join in on and learn the ropes.

What should I expect?

You should expect anything and everything. Dungeons and Dragons is a completely open world game, there the only limits is what the DM will allow you to do.

How do I read dice rolls?

Whenever you are supposed to roll a die or a set of dice, your DM will most likely tell you to roll xdy dice where x stands for the number of dice you'll roll and y stand for how many sides on that dice. For example, if the DM said roll 3d6 you would roll a 6 sided die 3 times.

What books should I get?

There are three main books that will help you get started in the D&D world. The Dungeon Master's Guide: This book teached you how to run the game. The Player's Handbook: this one teaches you how to play the game. The Monster Manual: this book is filled with monsters and potential encounters.
